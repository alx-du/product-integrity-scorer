Executive summary + methods

A small set of current alternatives meaningfully reduce landfill contribution and chemical exposure versus conventional disposables according to the available evidence: hybrid/reusable systems built around reusable waterproof shells and cloth inserts, compostable or biobased disposable systems with industrial take-back, and higher-transparency reduced-chemistry disposables.

The scoring rubric ranks GroVia hybrid highest overall, followed by Dyper, Eco by Naty / Kudos, Moltex, and Bambo Nature. Key constraints include limited industrial composting access, uneven independent chemical testing across brands, and variable on-bench performance among eco-branded disposables.

How to read this: the score is not a universal “best diaper” claim. It is a structured comparison of tradeoffs: landfill reduction, chemical transparency, value, performance, feasible disposal, and governance credibility.

Scoring rubric

Criteria are weighted to balance household value, environmental impact, chemical safety, performance, end-of-life feasibility, and corporate governance. Scores run from 0–100 per criterion, and the weighted sum yields the overall score.
